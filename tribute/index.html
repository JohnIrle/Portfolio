Nikola Tesla

Inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, physicist, and futurist

A time line of major events.

  • 1856 - Born into a Serb family in the village of Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia).
  • 1881 - Allocated the chief electrician of the Budapest Telephone Exchange. During his employment, Tesla made many improvements to the Central Station equipment and claimed to have perfected a telephone repeater or amplifier, which was never patented nor publicly described.
  • 1882 - Begins working for the Continental Edison Company in France, designing and making improvements to electrical equipment.
  • 1884 - relocated to New York City where he was hired by Thomas Edison to work at his Edison Machine Works on Manhattan's lower east side.
  • 1885 - offered $50,000 to completely redesigning the Edison Company's direct current generators. Quit after Edison told him he was "only joking" and that Tesla "didn't understand American humor."
  • 1886 - Tesla Electric Light & Manufacturing opens. The company installed electrical arc light based illumination systems designed by Tesla and also had designs for dynamo electric machine commutators, the first patents issued to Tesla in the US.
  • 1887 - Tesla Electric Company opens.
  • 1888 - Tesla's Alternating current induction moter is patented. "War of Currents" is started between Tesla's claim on Alternating current and Edison's claim on Direct Current.
  • 1891 - Tesla becomes an American Citizen.
  • 1893 - Westinghouse shows the American public the safety of Alternating current at the World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago, under the name of "Tesla Polyphase System"
  • 1895 - Looses $50,000 worth of plans, notes, lab equipment and photographs in a labritory fire.
  • 1896 - Begins experimenting with x-rays and devises an x-ray generator that uses his Tesla coil.
  • 1898 - Demonstrates a radio-controlled boat to the public during an electrical exhibition at Madison Square Garden.
  • 1900 - Granted patents for a "system of transmitting electrical energy" and "an electrical transmitter."
  • 1901 - Builds WardenClyffe tower
  • 1906 - Tesla demonstrates his 200 horsepower, 16,000 rpm bladeless turbine
  • 1919 - begins working for Allis-Chalmers in Milwaukee.
  • 1928 - receives last patent for a biplane capable of taking of vertically.
